A Letter From Winston

Why I'm Sharing This

This is my fourth year at Abundance360. Every year I come for the same reason: to be around people who are genuinely looking around the corner — people who can see what's coming and are building toward it rather than away from it. That's rare. I don't take it for granted.

When ChatGPT arrived, I recognized it immediately as one of the most important moments in human history. I knew there would be winners and losers, and I knew which side of that trade I had to be on. That belief is what brought me to this room four years ago. This year, I watched every prediction come true — faster than even the optimists expected.

The singularity is here. Not coming. Here. AI is now self-replicating and growing exponentially. We are living through what scientists and futurists have been describing for decades, and most of society still hasn't fully registered it.

2026 is the Year of the Agent. Think Tony Stark and Jarvis — an AI assistant customized to you, running 24 hours a day, capable of acting autonomously across any domain. I had a business conversation this week about integrating games at one of our entertainment venues. I found myself thinking: if we both had agents running alongside that conversation, mine would have tracked our customer segmentation needs while his catalogued their capabilities and lowest-cost options. We'd have left with a business plan, not just a goal to collaborate. That moment is this year. Not five years from now.

The robotics wave is right behind it. Humanoid robots are 2 to 3 years from mass adoption. Speakers at Abundance360 projected roughly one billion robots operating in society within five years — a bold forecast, but one coming from people who have been right before. Real disruption ahead — but also real solutions to labor shortages, elder care, and the crisis of loneliness we're not talking about enough.

Bill Gross said something I can't shake: "The greatest risk right now is not AI. It's not turning to AI." Use AI as a mirror. Use it to challenge your thinking, ask harder questions about your decisions, and show you what you're missing. That is its highest use.

I'll be honest: there are moments I go down dark holes thinking about where this leads. And moments of wild optimism. Both are valid. The people I spend this week with care deeply about making AI beneficial for humanity. They talk about the risks openly. That matters to me.

We live in a world where science fiction is becoming reality at a pace that is hard to fathom. It is terrifying. It is overwhelming. It is also the greatest moment for value creation I have seen in my lifetime. The only answer is to become fluent. Run, don't walk. The question isn't whether you should engage with AI. The question is how fast you can start. Everything that follows in this report is my attempt to help you answer that.

Winston Fisher

The State of AI · 2026

We Have Crossed a Threshold

This is not incremental progress. The pace of change has reached a point where waiting to engage is itself a strategy — a losing one.

"We are at the knee of the exponential curve. The next 18 months will produce more change than the last 10 years combined." — Peter Diamandis, Abundance360, 2026

I spent this week surrounded by some of the most forward-thinking minds in business, science, and technology at Abundance360 — Peter Diamandis's annual gathering of people committed to understanding and shaping the future. What I heard was not hype. It was a sober, data-driven, and at times uncomfortable look at what is already happening — and what is coming next.

My honest takeaway: There is disruption coming that will touch every industry. But disruption has always created winners. The difference between prospering and struggling in the next three years comes down to one question: Are you on the side of the change, or the side being changed?
2023–2024
The Conversation Phase
AI could talk to you, write for you, answer questions. Impressive, but every action still required a human to take it somewhere. Useful as a tool, limited as a collaborator.
2025
AI Learns to Reason
Models like OpenAI o3, Google Gemini 2.0, and Anthropic Claude 3.7 Sonnet gained the ability to think through complex problems. They began outperforming human experts in medicine, law, and engineering on standardized benchmarks. This was the moment the tool became a collaborator.
2026 — Now
The Year of AI Agents
AI can now take actions, not just answer questions. Agents browse the web, write and execute code, manage workflows, book meetings, analyze documents, and complete multi-step tasks on your behalf. This is the year companies begin deploying AI workforces — not pilots, real operations. A single human can now direct what previously required a team of ten.
2027 — The Separation
Winners Emerge. Losers Become Visible.
AI-native businesses will operate at 3–5× the productivity of traditional competitors. The first measurable wave of white-collar job displacement will appear in economic data. New categories of work will emerge — but the transition will be painful for those who are unprepared. This is my prediction, not a certainty — but the trend lines are clear.
2028–2030
Physical AI and Abundance
Humanoid robots (Tesla Optimus, Figure, 1X) move from factories into service roles. AI-driven drug discovery produces major health breakthroughs. Longevity science becomes actionable. The cost of intelligence, labor, and computation continues its collapse. For those positioned on the right side of this curve, genuine abundance becomes accessible.
The Defining Concept · 2026

What Is an AI Agent?

This term will define the next two years. Understanding it will help you see both the opportunity and the threat clearly.

💬

Old AI: The Chatbot

You ask a question. AI answers. You copy the answer and do something with it. Every action still required a human. Useful — but the leverage was limited. You were the worker; AI was the assistant.

⚡

New AI: The Agent

You give AI a goal. It breaks the task into steps, takes actions on your behalf — reads documents, searches the web, writes and runs code, sends emails, updates systems — and reports back. You are the director. AI is the workforce.

🔗

Multi-Agent Systems

Companies are now deploying teams of AI agents that work in parallel: one researches, one writes, one checks compliance, one executes. A single human can direct the output of what once required a department.

What this means practically right now: A real estate analyst using AI agents today can do the work of five analysts. A financial advisor with AI tools can serve three times the clients. A solo entrepreneur can run operations that previously required a full team. The leverage is real, it is available today, and it costs less than one employee's monthly salary to access.

The Hard Truth

The billable hours model is under structural pressure. Firms that bill by the hour for work AI can do in minutes will lose clients to those that bill for outcomes. The professional who adapts becomes significantly more valuable. The professional who ignores this becomes structurally expensive.

The Counterintuitive Truth

What Becomes More Valuable

AI doesn't make humans irrelevant. It makes certain human capabilities extraordinarily more valuable. Here's where to invest in yourself.

🧭

Judgment & Discernment

AI produces output at scale. Knowing what to do with it — what's right, what's wrong, what matters — is entirely human. The person with good judgment directing AI is 10× more powerful than AI without direction.

🤝

Authentic Relationships

Trust, rapport, empathy, and genuine human connection are the one thing AI cannot provide. As transactional roles get automated, the relationship-builder commands a premium that compounds over time.

🎯

Creative Direction

Knowing what to create — the right idea, the right frame, the right question — is more valuable than knowing how to execute it. AI handles execution. Taste, vision, and originality remain human.

⚡

Learning Velocity

The single most valuable skill of the next decade is the ability to continuously learn new tools and approaches. People who are comfortable with discomfort will compound their advantage every year.

🏛️

Ethics & Accountability

AI cannot be held responsible. Humans who can navigate ethical complexity and stand behind decisions will be essential in every organization deploying AI at scale. This is not a soft skill — it's a strategic asset.

🚀

Entrepreneurial Thinking

Identifying problems worth solving, taking ownership of outcomes, and building something from nothing — these remain deeply human. AI is the most powerful tool entrepreneurs have ever had access to.

Honest Assessment

The Real Risks

Not hype. Not fear-mongering. These are grounded concerns that deserve serious attention. Knowing them clearly is part of navigating this well.

High Concern

White-Collar Job Displacement

Unlike previous automation that primarily affected manufacturing, AI targets knowledge work — analysis, writing, research, customer service, coding, legal work. The timeline is 2–5 years for significant disruption in many professional fields. This is not speculation; it is already measurable in hiring trends at major firms. New jobs will emerge, but the transition period will be painful for those who haven't prepared.

High Concern

AI-Powered Fraud and Impersonation

Deepfake audio and video now enable convincing impersonations of family members, executives, and financial advisors. Voice cloning takes under 30 seconds of sample audio. Real-time deepfake video calls are commercially available. Financial fraud using these tools is growing rapidly. Use code words with family members before acting on any financial request. Always verify through a separate, known channel. If something feels wrong, it may be.

High Concern

Concentration of Power

The frontier of AI development sits with a handful of companies — OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta, xAI — and the governments backing them. The economic and geopolitical advantages this creates are profound and growing. Most citizens, small businesses, and governments are largely passengers on a trajectory being set by a small number of actors whose incentives may not align with broad societal benefit.

Significant Concern

Information Integrity

AI-generated misinformation — news articles, social posts, expert analysis, product reviews — is becoming indistinguishable from authentic content at scale. The challenge to shared reality this creates is unprecedented. Develop the habit of source verification. Be skeptical of emotionally activating content. The truth is still out there — finding it requires more effort than it used to.

Significant Concern

Family, Young People, and AI Companions

AI companions and tutors are available now and growing rapidly. The long-term developmental implications for young people — social, emotional, cognitive — are genuinely unknown. There is reasonable cause for deliberate family judgment here that the market will not exercise on your behalf. Engagement with AI is not the same as engagement with the world.

Significant Concern

Cybersecurity at Every Level

AI-powered cyberattacks are more sophisticated, cheaper, and more targeted than anything that came before. Small businesses and individuals that were previously low-value targets are now accessible. Basic cybersecurity hygiene — password managers, two-factor authentication, regular backups — is no longer optional for anyone.

Monitor Closely

Regulatory and Legal Uncertainty

Governments worldwide are writing AI policy in real time. Early-adopting businesses face exposure — liability for AI decisions, data use violations, employment law — that the regulatory environment hasn't fully defined. Build AI integration with flexibility. Today's competitive advantage can become tomorrow's compliance problem. Stay close to this as it develops.

Your Path: Starting Strong

Everyone who is expert at this started exactly where you are. The goal right now is not to master AI — it is to stop being afraid of it. Here is your honest, simple path forward.

This Week: Just Start
  • Go to claude.ai or chat.openai.com and create a free account. That's the entire first step.
  • Ask it something you actually want to know about. Don't test it — use it like a smart colleague.
  • Ask it to help you write one email or draft one document this week. See what happens.
  • Watch Sal Khan's 15-minute TED talk on AI and education. It's genuinely reassuring.
This Month: Build the Habit
  • Use AI to help with something every single day — even just summarizing an article.
  • Ask it about your specific field: "What are the biggest ways AI is affecting [your industry]?"
  • Share what you learn with one other person. Teaching is the fastest way to solidify understanding.
The key mindset shift: AI is not a search engine. It's more like a conversation with a very knowledgeable collaborator who has read almost everything. The better you get at asking it things — what practitioners call "prompting" — the more useful it becomes. You are training yourself, not the AI.

Your Path: Accelerate and Focus

You understand what AI can do. The next step is moving from occasional user to someone who has genuinely integrated these tools — and understanding where the real leverage points are in your specific situation.

High-Impact Moves This Month
  • Identify the 3 most repetitive cognitive tasks in your week. Build a simple AI workflow for each one.
  • Learn basic prompt structure: Context → Task → Format → Constraints. This framework makes AI 3× more useful immediately.
  • Try a specialized tool in your industry — not just ChatGPT, but something built for your field.
  • Set up a "second brain" workflow: use AI to summarize and retrieve information from what you read.
Tools Worth Exploring
  • Perplexity.ai — AI-powered research with live citations. Better than Google for complex questions.
  • Notion AI or Obsidian with AI plugins — knowledge management with AI built in.
  • Make.com or Zapier AI — automate repetitive tasks without writing code.
  • Claude Projects — give it context about your business and have ongoing, contextual conversations.
The question to ask yourself: "If I had an extremely capable collaborator who could work 24/7, never tired, and could research, write, and analyze at a high level — what would I give them?" That's your AI. The bottleneck is no longer capability. It's your ability to direct it well.

Your Path: Build Competitive Moats

You have the foundation. The window to build a meaningful competitive advantage using AI is open right now — and it will not stay open long. The question is what you build in the next 12 months.

Where the Real Leverage Is in 2026
  • AI agents: Stop using AI as a tool. Start deploying it as a workforce. Identify 3 processes that could be fully handed to an agent.
  • Proprietary data layers: The companies that win long-term own unique, structured data their AI learns from. What data do you have that nobody else does?
  • Vertical AI tools: General models are commoditizing. Industry-specific implementations trained on your workflows — that's where the margin lives.
  • Talent strategy: Hire for AI literacy. Your next five hires should understand how to work with AI, not in spite of it.
The Question to Answer Now
Ask yourself this: "If an AI-native competitor entered my market tomorrow — no legacy systems, no old processes, full access to modern AI — what would they do differently? How long before they had a structural cost or quality advantage over me?" If the answer is less than 18 months, you have your roadmap. Build that competitor yourself, before someone else does.

AI Readiness · 2026

Questions You Should Be Asking

These are not rhetorical. Set aside 30 minutes this month and work through each one honestly. Your answers will tell you what to do next.

01

What is the first role in my organization that AI could do better than my next hire?

Not should — could. Be honest about this. If you lead a team, this is the most strategically important question you have right now.

02

Am I preparing my family and the young people in my life for a world where their competition includes AI?

What skills are you actively reinforcing that compound in an AI world? What assumptions about education and career are you operating on that may no longer hold?

03

What is my personal "unfair advantage" that AI genuinely cannot replicate?

Your network, your judgment, your relationships, your domain depth. Every person has something. Have you identified yours clearly enough to build on it?

04

Is my business model resilient to a competitor operating at 50% of my cost using AI?

This is already happening in some industries. What happens to your economics if a well-funded competitor automates what you currently employ people to do?

05

What does my personal data landscape look like — and am I comfortable with it?

How much of your information is already in AI systems? Most people have no clear picture of their digital footprint. Now is the time to get one.

06

Who in my life is most vulnerable to this disruption, and what can I do to help?

Employees, children, parents, friends — the disruption will not be evenly distributed. Who needs information and support that you are positioned to provide?

07

If I had to become genuinely fluent in AI in 90 days, what would that plan actually look like?

Not a casual user — genuinely fluent. What would you commit to? Who would you learn from? What would you build or experiment with?

08

Am I investing in the AI wave, not just adapting to it?

This will create enormous wealth — most of it flowing to platforms and the people who positioned themselves early. Are you thinking about where you want to be financially, not just professionally?
